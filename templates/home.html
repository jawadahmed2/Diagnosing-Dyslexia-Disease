Our AI Approach

Below are the AI Approach we are used to predict Dyslexia Disease

Deep Learning Approach

In our project, we're using advanced technology called deep learning. We rely on a powerful tool called TensorFlow, which helps us create smart computer models. These models are like brains that can learn from lots of information. With the help of TensorFlow, we analyze big sets of data, including brain scans, tests, and school results. We use these models to find hidden connections and clues about dyslexia, a reading difficulty. TensorFlow is like the engine that drives our project, helping us understand dyslexia better and find ways to help people who have it. We keep improving our methods to make sure our models can do their job well and help as many people as possible.

Machine Learning Approach

In our project, we also utilize machine learning techniques to understand dyslexia better. Unlike deep learning, which mimics the human brain's neural networks, machine learning focuses on algorithms that can learn from data and make predictions. We preprocess the data by normalizing it to a common scale and categorizing it into different classes. Then, we train various machine learning models using this data, such as Random Forest and Gradient Boosting classifiers. These models analyze patterns in the data and make predictions about dyslexia based on features extracted from brain scans and other tests. By evaluating the performance of these models and selecting the best one, we aim to provide accurate and reliable insights into dyslexia detection and diagnosis. Through the application of machine learning, we strive to improve our understanding of dyslexia and contribute to the development of effective interventions and support for individuals affected by this condition.

Documentation

Transfer Learning Approach

In our project, we leverage transfer learning, a powerful technique in the field of deep learning, to address the challenges of detecting dyslexia. Transfer learning involves taking a pre-trained neural network model and adapting it to a new, related task. By using a pre-trained model that has already learned useful features from a large dataset, we can significantly reduce the amount of labeled data needed for training, making it especially valuable for tasks with limited available data. In our case, we utilize pre-trained ResNet, which were originally trained on vast image datasets like ImageNet. We then fine-tune these models on our dyslexia dataset, allowing them to learn specific patterns and features relevant to dyslexia detection. By incorporating transfer learning, we aim to capitalize on the knowledge learned from large-scale datasets and adapt it to the unique characteristics of dyslexia, ultimately enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of our diagnostic system. Through this approach, we strive to make significant strides in the early detection and understanding of dyslexia, ultimately improving outcomes for affected individuals.

About Us

We are a dedicated team passionate about leveraging cutting-edge technology to address real-world challenges. Our project focuses on using advanced machine learning and deep learning techniques to tackle dyslexia, a common learning disorder affecting individuals' ability to read and comprehend text. By harnessing the power of artificial intelligence, we aim to develop innovative solutions for early detection and intervention, ultimately improving the lives of those affected by dyslexia. Our interdisciplinary team comprises experts in machine learning, neuroscience, and education, united by a shared commitment to making a positive impact in the field of neurodevelopmental disorders. With a data-driven approach and a focus on accessibility and inclusivity, we strive to empower individuals with dyslexia and their communities through technology-driven solutions.

Our Philosophy

At the heart of our project lies a profound belief in the transformative power of technology to drive positive change in society. We believe that every individual, regardless of their cognitive differences, deserves access to inclusive and personalized learning experiences. Our philosophy is rooted in empathy, understanding, and a commitment to harnessing the latest advancements in artificial intelligence and machine learning to address complex challenges such as dyslexia. We prioritize collaboration, innovation, and evidence-based practices, working hand in hand with educators, researchers, and individuals with dyslexia to co-create solutions that are effective, accessible, and empowering. Through continuous learning, adaptation, and a dedication to ethical and responsible use of technology, we strive to make meaningful contributions to the field of neurodevelopmental disorders, paving the way for a more inclusive and equitable future for all.
